Pterin-4-alpha-carbinolamine dehydratase is an enzyme that in humans is encoded by the PCBD1 gene. == Function == This gene encodes pterin-4 alpha- carbinolamine dehydratase, an enzyme involved in phenylalanine hydroxylation. The enzyme regulates the homodimerization of the transcription factor hepatocyte nuclear factor 1 (HNF1). ==Clinical significance== Mutations of the PCBD1 gene cause pterin-4 alpha-carbinolamine dehydratase deficiency, one of the forms of tetrahydrobiopterin deficiency. == Interactions == PCBD1 has been shown to interact with DYRK1B and HNF1A. == References == == Further reading == * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
